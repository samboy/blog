Famous Chess games

These are famous Chess games which I refer to in other articles. The games are shown here without commentary.

White here is Karpov, who was world champion at the time. Black is Tony Miles, England’s first Grandmaster, who was quite a character. His life was sadly cut short at the age of 46 in 2001. It’s Only Me is a touching biography that honors Miles’ life.

Short v. Kasparov, 1993 (Exhibition game)

1. e4 e5 2. f4 exf4 3. Bc4 Qh4+ 4. Kf1 b5 5. Bxb5 Nf6 6. Nf3 Qh6 7. Nc3 g5 8. d4 Bb7 9. h4 Rg8 10. Kg1 gxh4 11. Rxh4 Qg6 12. Qe2 Nxe4 13. Rxf4 f5 14. Nh4 Qg3 15. Nxe4 1-0

This was a series of themed games, where the tournament organizers told the players what opening they had to play. After two drawn games, Kasparov was asked to play the Black side of the very dubious Bryan countergambit, and quickly lost.
